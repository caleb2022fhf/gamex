🎱 8-Ball Pool Game

Essential Equipment

  • Pool Table – 7 ft, 8 ft, or 9 ft.
  • Pool Balls Set
    • 15 numbered balls
    • 1 white cue ball
  • Pool Cues (sticks) – At least 2.
  • Triangle Rack
  • Chalk
  • Table Brush

🏠 Space Requirements

  • Enough space to move the cue stick comfortably.
  • An 8-ft table needs about 13 x 17 feet (4 x 5.2 meters).

💡 Recommended Extras

  • Overhead pool table light
  • Cue rack (wall or floor)
  • Extra cue tips
  • Table cover

📜 8-Ball Pool – Basic Rules

1️⃣ Setup

  • Rack 15 balls in a triangle.
  • 8-ball in the center.
  • Solid in one bottom corner, stripe in the other.
  • Cue ball starts behind the head line.

2️⃣ The Break

  • One player breaks.
  • If a ball goes in, player continues.
  • If no ball goes in, turn passes.

3️⃣ Choose Your Group

  • First legally pocketed ball decides group:
    • Solids (1–7)
    • Stripes (9–15)

4️⃣ Taking Turns

  • Must hit your group first.
  • If you pocket your ball, continue.
  • If you miss or foul, turn ends.

5️⃣ Common Fouls

  • Hitting opponent’s ball first
  • Not hitting any ball
  • Scratch (cue ball in pocket)
  • No ball hits rail after contact

Penalty: Opponent gets ball in hand.

6️⃣ Winning

  • Pocket all your group balls first.
  • Then legally pocket the 8-ball.
  • Pocket 8-ball early → You lose.
  • Scratch on 8-ball → You lose.
