Essential Equipment 🏠

  1. Pool Table – Like the one in the image (most common sizes: 7 ft, 8 ft, or 9 ft).
  2. Pool Balls Set – Standard 16-ball set: 15 numbered balls + 1 white cue ball
  3. Pool Cues (sticks) – Usually at least 2.
  4. Triangle Rack – To arrange the balls at the start.
  5. Chalk – Helps prevent the cue tip from slipping.
  6. Table Brush – To clean the felt surface.

Space Requirements

You need enough room around the table to move the cue stick comfortably.

For example: An 8-ft table typically needs about 13 x 17 feet (4 x 5.2 meters) of room.

Recommended Extras 💡

  • Overhead pool table light
  • Cue rack (wall or floor)
  • Extra cue tips
  • Table cover (to protect from dust)

8-Ball Pool – Basic Rules

  1. Setup 🏃
    Rack the 15 balls in a triangle. The 8-ball goes in the center. A solid ball in one bottom corner, a stripe in the other. The white ball (cue ball) starts behind the head line.
  2. The Break 💥
    One player breaks (hits the racked balls first). If a ball goes in, that player continues. If no ball goes in, the turn passes.
  3. Choose Your Group 🎯
    After the break: The first legally pocketed ball decides your group: Solids (1–7) or Stripes (9–15). The other player gets the remaining group.
  4. Taking Turns 🔄
    On your turn: You must hit your group first. If you pocket one of your balls, you continue. If you miss or commit a foul, your turn ends.
  5. Fouls (Common Ones) ❌
    Hitting opponent’s ball first / Not hitting any ball / Cue ball goes into a pocket (scratch) / No ball hits a rail after contact
    If you foul → opponent gets ball in hand (they can place the cue ball anywhere).
  6. Winning the Game 🏆
    After pocketing all your group balls, you must legally pocket the 8-ball. If you pocket the 8-ball too early → you lose. If you scratch while pocketing the 8-ball → you lose.
